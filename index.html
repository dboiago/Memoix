Recipe management built from the line

Memoix

An offline-first tool for managing recipes, notes, and culinary reference material.

Google Play Android GitHub Releases Windows • Sideload

Memoix has no opinion about your cloud storage. It does not require an account or background connection.

Your recipes are yours. This is not a revolutionary concept.

Import

Photograph a cookbook page, paste a URL, drop in raw text, or open a share link. Memoix normalises units, title-cases names, and converts fractions to symbols before you review a single line. A review screen lets you adjust, tag, and place the recipe before it saves.

Works with most major recipe sites. Reads handwritten notebooks. Handles imperial and metric.

Cooking

Side-by-side mode keeps ingredients and directions on screen together, each column scrolling independently. Mark steps complete as you work. Long-press any timed step to start a timer that keeps running even in the background. Step images open inline. Ingredient quantities scale by long-pressing the serving size.

Structure

Recipes organise by course. Component dishes use linked sub-recipes that can scale independently. Pair a main with its sides. Detail screens show what a recipe is paired with, and what links back to it. Meal plans arrange by day and course and can be reorganised by dragging.

Sharing

Export any recipe as a share link or QR code. Scan the code in Memoix to import directly. No internet required. No account on either end.

This is by design, not oversight.
